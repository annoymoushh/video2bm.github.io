Video2BM: Can Leveraging Human Videos Enhance Performance and Generalizability in Bimanual Manipulation?

Annoymous

Motivation of Video2BM

Abstract

Bimanual manipulation (BM) is a critical challenge in robotic learning, requiring precise coordination of two arms for complex tasks. However, training robots for BM relies heavily on costly and time-consuming robot demonstration data. In contrast, human videos, being more cost-effective and easier to collect, have gained significant attention. This paper explores whether leveraging human videos can enhance performance and generalizability in BM. We propose Video2BM, a framework that uses human demonstration videos as a scalable alternative to robot data. By transferring knowledge from human to robot domains, Video2BM reduces reliance on expensive robot data while maintaining task performance. Central to our approach is the Temporal-Spatial Alignment (TSA) module, which ensures consistency between human and robot demonstrations. Video2BM not only lowers the barrier to robotic learning but also advances the development of versatile and adaptive robotic systems. Extensive experiments show that Video2BM offers three key advantages: (1) Performance Improvement: Human videos significantly boost performance across diverse long-horizon BM tasks at varying difficulty levels. (2) Enhanced Generalization: Human videos improve generalization capabilities, including object, viewpoint, and positional generalization. (3) Human Video Scaling Law: Video2BM effectively leverages the efficiency of human data collection, exhibiting a pronounced scaling effect that substantially boosts task performance beyond what is achievable with robot data alone. Video2BM introduces a novel paradigm in robotic learning, paving the way for future bimanual manipulation research.
